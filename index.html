Research

I am broadly interested in applying machine learning to robotic planning and control systems, in order to (1) improve the capability and scientific value of small, inexpensive spacecraft built with commodity hardware; (2) improve the throughput, workflow, and scientific return of existing spacecraft missions.

I work on the following missions:

  • Lunar Polar Hydrogen Mapper (LunaH-Map): This 6U CubeSat mission was recently selected by NASAâ€™s Science Mission Directorate to fly as a secondary payload on first Exploration Mission (EM-1) of the Space Launch System (SLS), scheduled to launch in July 2018. The mission is led by Arizona State University (PI: Dr. Craig Hardgrove) and I'm the lead systems and software engineer. LunaH-Map is being designed to fly a pair of neutron spectrometers to determine hydrogen abundances at the South Pole of the Moon at scales below 10 km in order to understand the relationship between volatiles and permanently shadowed regions.
  • Mars Science Laboratory (aka Curiosity): I work on tactical software tools, e.g. compression artifact detection, for Mastcam.
